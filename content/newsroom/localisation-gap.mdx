We pulled 4,000 club posts across nine languages and split them into two groups: captions translated from a source-market post, and captions written natively for the market they landed in.

The native-written group outperformed on every engagement metric we measured. The gap was widest in markets with strong regional slang and narrowest in markets closest to the source language.

### What this means for a content plan

Translation gets the words right. It doesn't get the register right, and register is most of what makes a fan stop scrolling. A market editor writing from scratch, briefed on the same news, consistently beats a translated version of the original post.
